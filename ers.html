What is the Elo Rating System?

The Elo rating system is a method for calculating the relative skill levels of players in two-player games such as chess. Developed by Arpad Elo, it is widely used by chess federations around the world.

How It Works

  • Each player has a rating, typically starting around 1200.
  • When two players compete, the expected outcome is calculated based on their ratings.
  • After the game, ratings are adjusted depending on the result and the expected score.
  • Winning against a stronger opponent gains more points; losing to a weaker one loses more.
